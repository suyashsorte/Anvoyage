Floating in splendid isolation, away from the Indian mainland lies a group of 572 islands, islets and rocks known as Andaman and Nicobar Islands. It is the union territory of India which stretches over an area of 700 square kilometers. It consists of 36 inhabited islands from north to south. These undulating islands are covered with dense forests and the various number of exotic birds and flowers. These islands were once a hill range which extended from Myanmar to Indonesia. The sandy beaches on the edge of flexuous coastline covered with coconut-palms undulate to the rhythm of the sea. These islands are hilly covered with evergreen forests. These islands are surrounded by the sea which offers great scope for water sports. It offers dream-view of rare gifts of nature as these islands are blessed with rare flora and fauna, with crystal clear water and mangrove-lined creeks, underwater marine life, and coral. With clean and wide roads, free of filth thanks to Swachh Bharat Abhiyan and fresh air is a dream destination for a nature lover who needs peace and tranquility. The main attractions are its adventure tourism like trekking, snorkeling, scuba diving, island camping, etc which gives you a memory of a lifetime.

The name Andaman is presumed to be derived from the mythological god Hanuman who was known as Handuman. These islands were the home of aboriginal tribes since prehistoric times. Some of the tribes are Great Andamanese, Onges, Jarawas, and Sentinelese which are of Negrito origin. The tribes of Nicobars are the Nicobarese and Shompens are of Mongoloid stock.

There are people from various religious beliefs who live together here. Hindu, Muslim, Sikhs, Christians and a couple of more religions can be found here. Variety of languages can also be found like Hindi, Bengali, Malayalam, Tamil, Telugu, etc. This land of a variety of people, a variety of people and languages is known as mini- India.

There are 92% of the land is forest and out of that 86% is reserved and protected forest land. About 50% of the land is used as National Parks, Wildlife Sanctuaries and tribal reserves.
